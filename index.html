Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to common questions about solar energy solutions

How much does solar panel installation cost?

+

Solar panel installation costs vary based on your home size, energy needs, and chosen system. We offer free consultations to provide accurate pricing tailored to your specific requirements. Most residential installations range from $15,000 to $40,000 before incentives, with significant savings available through federal and state tax credits.

How long do solar panels last?

+

Quality solar panels typically last 25-30 years with minimal maintenance. Most come with 20-25 year warranties, ensuring long-term energy savings and environmental benefits. After 25 years, panels may still produce 80-85% of their original capacity.

What areas does EcoSunline serve?

+

EcoSunline serves residential and commercial customers throughout California and neighboring states. Our service area includes Los Angeles, San Francisco, San Diego, Sacramento, and surrounding regions. Contact us to confirm service availability in your specific area.

Are government subsidies available?

+

Yes! The federal solar Investment Tax Credit (ITC) allows you to deduct 30% of the cost of installing a solar energy system from your federal taxes. Many states and local governments also offer additional incentives, rebates, and financing programs to make solar more affordable.

What's the process for getting started?

+

Getting started is simple: 1) Schedule a free consultation, 2) We assess your property and energy needs, 3) Receive a customized proposal, 4) Finalize financing and permits, 5) Professional installation, 6) System activation and monitoring. The entire process typically takes 4-8 weeks from consultation to activation.

Is solar energy suitable for commercial buildings?

+

Absolutely! Commercial solar installations are highly beneficial for businesses, offering significant cost savings, tax advantages, and environmental benefits. We specialize in large-scale commercial projects and can design systems for warehouses, office buildings, retail spaces, and manufacturing facilities.
